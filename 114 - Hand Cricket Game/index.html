How to Play Hand Cricket
Objective:
Score more runs than the computer or dismiss the computer player.
Player:
You will play against the computer.
Instructions:
1. Batting:
Choose a number between 1 and 6.
The computer will also choose a number between 1 and 6.
If your numbers match, you're out. Otherwise, your runs are added to your total score.
There is no limit to the number of balls (guesses) per inning.
2. Bowling (Computer's turn):
The computer tries to guess your chosen number.
If the computer guesses correctly, you're out. Otherwise, your runs are added to your total score.
3. Switch Roles:
After each inning (when you get out or score runs), the computer bats and you bowl.
4. Scoring:
Runs are based on the numbers chosen by you and the computer.
If you're out, no runs are scored for that inning.
5. Winner:
The player with the highest total score wins.
